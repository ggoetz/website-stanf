Photovoltaic Retinal Prosthesis for Restoring Sight to the Blind

Retinal degenerative diseases such as retinitis pigmentosa or age-related macular degeneration are among the leading causes of blindness in the developped world. In patients suffering from these conditions, the light-sensitive photoreceptors in the retina slowly die off, eventually resulting in blindness. However, many of the inner retinal neurons which process and transmit visual invormation are preserved, and electrically stimulating them can elicit phosphenes (percepts of lights).

This idea led to a first generation of retinal implants, pioneered by Second Sight and Retina Implant AG. Building upon this technology, the Palanker Lab is developing a high-resolution, highly scalable, fully optical subretinal prosthetic implant which aims at efficiently delivering visual information to degenerate retinas while conserving the natural coupling between eye movement and scanning of a visual scene in a package simple to implant surgically.

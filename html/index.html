About me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the department of Electrical Engineering at Stanford University, where I study retinal prostheses for sight restoration in people suffering from degenerative diseases of the retina, such as retinitis pigmentosa or age-related macular degeneration. I am part of the Palanker lab at Stanford and also work in close collaboration with the Sher lab at UCSC. The Stanford photovoltaic retinal implant is now being developped in collaboration with Pixium Vision, a start-up company based at the Vision Institute in Paris, France.

Prior to Stanford, I obtained a diplôme d'ingénieur from Ecole Centrale Paris, France, where my focus was on Applied Mathematics.
